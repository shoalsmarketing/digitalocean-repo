Step-by-Step: Setting Up Your WebinarJam Trial

1Accessing the Trial Offer

Where to Start: Navigate to the WebinarJam trial page through their official website or promotional links.

  • Look for "Start Trial" or "$1 Trial" buttons on the homepage
  • Review the trial terms (duration, features included, cancellation policy)
  • Ensure you understand billing: $1 upfront, then cancels or converts to paid plan

What You'll Need: Email address, payment method (for $1 trial charge), business information (optional but helpful for testing)

2Creating Your Account

Registration Process:

  • Enter your email address and create a secure password
  • Provide payment information (even for $1 trial)
  • Verify your email address through the confirmation link
  • Complete your profile with business details

Pro Tip: Use your actual business email and details during trial setup. This lets you test the registration experience from your audience's perspective and ensures proper email deliverability testing.

3Dashboard Orientation

Upon first login, you'll see the WebinarJam dashboard. Take 10 minutes to explore:

  • Left Navigation: Main menu for webinars, recordings, settings, integrations
  • Create Webinar Button: Primary action to start new events
  • Active Webinars: Shows your scheduled and live events
  • Quick Stats: Registration, attendance, and engagement metrics
  • Help Resources: Access to tutorials, documentation, support

4Creating Your First Test Webinar

Click "Create New Webinar" and follow the setup wizard:

Basic Information:

  • Webinar title (use a test name like "Trial Webinar - Do Not Register")
  • Description and presenter information
  • Choose webinar type: Live, Automated, or Hybrid

Schedule Settings:

  • Set a date/time at least 2 hours in the future for testing
  • Choose timezone (critically important for audience experience)
  • Set duration (start with 30 minutes for testing)

Registration Page:

  • Choose a template or create custom design
  • Customize colors, images, and copy
  • Add countdown timer (test this feature)
  • Configure registration fields (name, email minimum)

5Configuring Email Notifications

Set up automated emails (this is where WebinarJam shines):

  • Confirmation email: Sent immediately after registration
  • Reminder sequence: 24 hours, 1 hour, 15 minutes before event
  • Join link delivery: Include webinar access link
  • Replay notification: Post-event follow-up

Testing Tip: Register yourself with 2-3 different email addresses to receive and review all automated messages. Check formatting, links, and deliverability.

6Setting Up Integrations

Connect your marketing tools during the trial to test real-world workflow:

  • Email marketing: Connect MailChimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, etc.
  • CRM integration: Sync registrant data to your CRM
  • Payment processors: If testing paid webinars, connect Stripe/PayPal
  • Analytics: Add Google Analytics or Facebook Pixel tracking

Why This Matters: Integration setup can be the most time-consuming aspect. Test it during the trial to ensure compatibility with your existing tools.

Conducting Your Trial Test Webinar

The most valuable part of your trial is running an actual test webinar. Here's how to get the most from it:

Pre-Webinar Testing Checklist

  • Test your camera and microphone in WebinarJam's green room
  • Verify screen sharing works with your presentation software
  • Practice switching between presenter video and screen share
  • Test the chat functionality and Q&A features
  • Create and test an offer/CTA injection
  • Set up and test polls or surveys
  • Invite 2-3 colleagues to join as test attendees
  • Check audio/video quality from attendee perspective

During Your Test Run

Features to Specifically Test:

  • Broadcast quality: How's the video/audio lag? Any buffering?
  • Chat moderation: Can you manage messages effectively during presentation?
  • Offer delivery: Test injecting timed offers with countdown
  • Attendee view: Join on mobile and desktop to see experience
  • Recording: Verify the session is being recorded properly
  • Analytics tracking: Check if engagement data is capturing correctly

Post-Webinar Review

After your test webinar, evaluate these critical elements:

  • Recording quality: Watch the replay—is it smooth and clear?
  • Analytics accuracy: Do the numbers match reality?
  • Email delivery: Did replay notifications send correctly?
  • CRM sync: Did attendee data flow to your connected tools?
  • User experience: Get feedback from test attendees

Key Features to Evaluate During Trial

Must-Test Features

  • Registration pages: Ease of creation, customization options, mobile responsiveness
  • Email automation: Reminder sequences, customization, deliverability
  • Live broadcasting: Video/audio quality, screen sharing, presentation tools
  • Engagement tools: Chat, Q&A, polls, offers, handouts
  • Recording & replay: Automatic recording, replay page creation, on-demand access
  • Analytics: Registration tracking, attendance rates, engagement metrics
  • Integrations: CRM, email marketing, payment processor connections
  • Multi-presenter: Test with 2+ presenters if you'll use this feature

Trial Evaluation: Is WebinarJam Right for You?

Before your trial ends, honestly answer these questions:

Decision Framework

  • Ease of use: Could you set up a webinar without constant help documentation?
  • Feature completeness: Does it have everything you need, or are critical features missing?
  • Reliability: Did broadcasts run smoothly without technical issues?
  • Integration compatibility: Did it connect seamlessly with your existing tools?
  • ROI potential: Can you realistically achieve results that justify the cost?
  • Support quality: Were help resources sufficient? Was support responsive?

After the Trial: Next Steps

If You're Continuing with WebinarJam

  • Choose the plan that matches your attendee capacity needs
  • Annual billing offers significant savings vs. monthly
  • Your trial webinars and settings typically carry over
  • Take advantage of onboarding resources for paid customers

If You're Canceling

  • Cancel before the trial period ends to avoid charges
  • Cancellation typically done through account settings or support
  • Export any data, recordings, or settings you want to keep
  • Document what you learned for future platform evaluations

Common Trial Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not testing with real scenarios: Use actual presentations, not generic test content
  • Skipping the attendee experience: Always join your own webinar as an attendee
  • Forgetting to test integrations: API connections are where issues often arise
  • Not inviting colleagues: Get feedback from presenters and attendees
  • Waiting until the last day: Start testing immediately to maximize trial value
  • Ignoring mobile experience: Many attendees join from phones/tablets

Getting Help During Your Trial

If you encounter issues or questions during your trial:

  • Knowledge base: Comprehensive articles on every feature
  • Video tutorials: Step-by-step setup guides
  • Email support: Usually responds within 24 hours
  • Live chat: Real-time assistance during business hours
  • Community forum: Learn from other users' experiences

Final Thoughts on the Trial Process

WebinarJam's trial approach—whether it's a $1 trial or money-back guarantee—removes most of the risk from your decision. The key is using that trial period strategically.

Don't just browse features. Run real tests. Invite real people. Use real presentations. The more closely your trial mirrors your actual use case, the more confident you'll be in your decision.

Remember: The trial period isn't about learning every advanced feature. It's about answering one question: Can this platform reliably deliver professional webinars that help achieve my business goals?

If the answer is yes, WebinarJam can become a valuable part of your marketing stack. If not, you've lost minimal time and money while gaining clarity about your requirements.
